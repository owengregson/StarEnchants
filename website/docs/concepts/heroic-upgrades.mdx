# Heroic upgrades

A **Heroic upgrade** is a one-shot power-up you apply to a single piece of gear — any armor piece or
weapon, *not* tied to a set. It is the high-roller item: a small success chance, consumed whether it
works or not, but a permanent boost when it lands.

On success a heroic upgrade:

- adds a **percent damage** bonus (for weapons),
- adds a **percent damage reduction** (for armor),
- adds a **durability** save chance (a chance to cancel item damage), and
- can optionally **upgrade the material** (e.g. diamond → netherite).

On failure the gear is unharmed — only the upgrade item is consumed.

## How heroic damage stacks

Heroic damage is an ordinary additive contribution: a heroic weapon's percent damage joins the same
additive outgoing bucket as any enchant, set, or crystal bonus, and a heroic armour piece's percent
reduction joins the additive reduction bucket. It carries no separate multiplier stage — it sums with
every other percent source and dilutes additively alongside them, just like a diamond-tier gear
bonus. (Heroic armour also grants real diamond-equivalent armour points and durability through the
vanilla stat system.)

:::tip Tune the risk
The success chance is drawn from a `[success-min, success-max]` range in the heroic item config
(defaults: 5–15%). Raise it for a more forgiving server, lower it for a true gamble.
:::
